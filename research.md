## Research Focus

My research focus has many different asects which helps me develop a comprehensive understanding of the field. I am particularly interested in the vision, visual attention, and our visual system behavior in real-world or laboratory settings. During my phd I have employed variety of tasks to understand temporal mechanisms in adaptaiton and improvement of visual system in different conditions including healthy and clinical populations. I have worked with amblyiopic patients, and also have tried to answer question not "where" but "when" is important in visual system. Both my master and PhD were funded by prestigious international olympiad of open-door where I became winner among over 1000 participants in 2019, and winner of Marie Curie fellowship in 2022.


I am a professiona programmer, a python developer and also I like JavaScript and R. Mostly I have developed my own tools for data analysis and also for open-science practices. I have contributed to JATOS project [JATOS](https://github.com/jatos/jatos); have developed many other packages which you can find in **My Tools** section of this website. 


I also love Mathematical modelling and application of math in vision and behavioral science.
